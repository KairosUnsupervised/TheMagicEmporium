# Player FAQ

---

## Item Rarities

Rarity determines how many modifiers an item can roll. Higher rarity means more modifiers, and access to stronger modifier slots. Tertiary modifiers are reserved for legendary items.

---

## Types of Modifiers

Each modifier belongs to one of four types, which determines how its strength is calculated and how multiple copies interact.

- **Unique** — A fixed effect that does not stack. If you have multiple items with the same Unique modifier, only the strongest copy applies
- **Linear** — Scales with a numeric value. Multiple copies add together, making the effect stronger the more items share it
- **Tiered** — Accumulates across items and unlocks qualitatively different tiers, having two Tier II equipped results in a Tier III effect. Powerful tiers may require cooperation between multiple items to reach
- **Independent** — Patches the item itself directly, adding abilities or properties to it rather than affecting your character sheet

---

## Modifier Strength — The Float

Every modifier roll comes with an internal float value between 0 and 1. This float determines which breakpoint activates, which controls the strength of the effect. Higher floats unlock stronger breakpoints, but strong breakpoints are rarer.

---

## Backgrounds

Some items are eligible to display a background image. Eligibility is determined by rarity, higher rarity items are more likely to qualify. When eligible, a strong modifier can provide the background that gets displayed on the item card.
